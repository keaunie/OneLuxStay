Popular Attractions in Redondo Beach

Stay close to Redondo’s iconic pier, harbor, coastal paths, and neighborhood favorites.

Redondo Beach Pier (Municipal Pier)

Redondo Beach’s most iconic landmark — a lively, horseshoe-shaped pier lined with ocean-view dining, boutique shops, fishing spots, and unforgettable sunsets over the Pacific.

Hopkins Wilderness Park

A lush retreat of forest trails, native plants, and quiet picnic spots across four unique ecological zones — a peaceful contrast to the vibrant coastline.

South Bay Bike Path (The Strand)

Ride the famous coastal bike trail stretching from Will Rogers State Beach through Redondo to Torrance Beach — passing piers, surf breaks, and classic beach cities.

Riviera Village

A charming, walkable district filled with boutiques, galleries, cozy cafés, and restaurants — a favorite local spot for slow strolls and sunset dinners.

South Bay Galleria

A three-story mall with 100+ stores, cinema, and diverse dining options — ideal for a shopping day just minutes from the coast.

Redondo Beach Historical Museum

A quaint museum housed in a preserved 1904 home, showcasing early seaside life, maritime history, and local stories that shaped Redondo.

The Bull Pen

A local institution since 1948, The Bull Pen serves classic steaks, cocktails, and live music in a warm neighborhood atmosphere.

Bluewater Grill

Dine seaside with fresh, sustainable seafood, harbor views, and a relaxed coastal vibe — perfect for lunch or dinner by the water.

King Harbor Marina

Watch sailboats drift by, enjoy waterfront dining, or rent a kayak to explore calm harbor waters — all from Redondo’s scenic marina.

Salt & Pearl

A stylish Riviera Village spot serving elevated seafood, steaks, and craft cocktails in a relaxed yet refined setting.

Manhattan Beach Pier

An iconic nearby pier just up the coast, perfect for day trips, dining, and classic Southern California ocean views.

Redondo Beach Transportation Options

Our Redondo Beach apartments are well connected to local transit, coastal paths, and regional hubs.

Fixed-Route Bus (Beach Cities Transit)

Local bus lines, such as Beach Cities Transit 102 & 109, connect Redondo Beach with nearby South Bay communities and transit connections.

Shared Ride Dial-A-Ride (Senior / Disabled Service)

Door-to-door ride service designed for seniors and persons with disabilities within the local area.

Bike Share / Bike Rentals

Pay-as-you-go bikes and rentals for casual coastal rides along The Strand and nearby paths.

Ride-Hailing / Taxi

On-demand services like Uber, Lyft, and local taxis provide flexible, point-to-point transportation throughout the South Bay and greater LA area.

Car Rental

Nearby rental providers offer vehicles for day trips, city exploration, and regional travel.

Electric Scooter / Micro-Mobility

Lightweight scooters and micro-mobility options are ideal for quick coastal commutes and short-distance errands.

Personal Cycling / Bike Path

Scenic bike paths run along the shoreline, connecting Redondo with neighboring beach cities.

Park & Ride / Carpool

Convenient park-and-ride options make it easy to share rides or connect with bus and rail services.

Electric Vehicle (EV) Travel / Charging

EV drivers benefit from multiple public charging stations throughout Redondo Beach and the South Bay.

Walking / Pedestrian Routes

A walkable seaside environment with pedestrian-friendly boulevards, oceanfront paths, and neighborhood streets.

Airport / Hotel Shuttle

Shared shuttles and private vans connect Redondo Beach with LAX and nearby transit centers.

Private Charter / Limousine Service

Premium chauffeured services and private charters available for events, business travel, or special occasions.
